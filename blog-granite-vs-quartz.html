Granite vs Quartz: Which Countertop Is Right for Your Steamboat Springs Home?

January 15, 2025 • 5 min read • By Steamboat Masters Granite

It is the most common question we hear from homeowners across the Yampa Valley: should I go with granite or quartz? After fabricating and installing over 1,500 kitchens and bathrooms, here is our honest take.

The Case for Granite

Granite is a natural stone quarried from the earth, which means every slab is genuinely one of a kind. If you value uniqueness and want a surface that no other home in Steamboat Springs will have, granite delivers.

It handles heat better than almost any other countertop material. In a mountain kitchen where cast iron comes straight from the stove to the counter, that matters. Granite is also extremely scratch resistant and, when properly sealed, resists stains from wine, coffee, and cooking oils.

The trade-off is annual maintenance. Granite requires periodic sealing — roughly once a year — to maintain its stain resistance. It is a simple process we teach every client during installation, but it is a step that quartz does not require.

The Case for Quartz

Quartz countertops are engineered from 93% natural quartz mixed with polymers and pigments. The result is a surface that is non-porous, stain-proof, and requires absolutely zero maintenance.

For busy families, vacation rental properties, or anyone who simply does not want to think about countertop care, quartz is hard to beat. It is also available in a massive range of colors and patterns, including designs that convincingly mimic Calacatta marble and Carrara marble without the fragility.

The downside? Quartz does not handle extreme heat as well as granite. Hot pans should be placed on trivets. And because it is engineered, it lacks the organic depth and uniqueness that only natural stone provides.

Our Recommendation

There is no wrong choice. Both materials are excellent investments that will last decades and increase your home value. Our advice is to let your lifestyle guide the decision.

If you cook with high heat regularly, love natural uniqueness, and do not mind a quick annual seal — choose granite. If you want a worry-free surface with consistent color and zero maintenance — choose quartz.

And if you are still not sure, that is exactly why we offer free consultations. We will bring samples to your home, show you how each material looks in your lighting, and help you make a decision you will love for years.
